If you've been Googling "how much does a website cost" — you've probably seen answers ranging from $500 to $50,000. That range is real, and it's confusing. Here's what Orange County businesses actually pay in 2026, and what drives the price difference.

## The Short Answer

For a professional custom website built for a local OC business, expect to invest **$1,500–$8,000** depending on scope. Here's how it breaks down:

**Basic Business Website (5-8 pages)**
$1,500–$3,500
Best for: service businesses, consultants, local shops
Includes: custom design, mobile-first build, basic on-page SEO, contact forms

**Growth-Focused Website (10-20 pages)**
$3,500–$7,000
Best for: businesses ready to compete in local search
Includes: everything above + location pages, blog setup, Google Analytics, conversion optimization

**E-Commerce or Complex Builds**
$6,000–$15,000+
Best for: online stores, booking platforms, membership sites

## Why Does Price Vary So Much?

**Template vs. Custom Design**
DIY builders like Wix or Squarespace run $15–$50/month but they look like everyone else and rank poorly. Custom-built sites take more time to build but perform significantly better in search.

**Local vs. Offshore**
You can find overseas developers for $500. You'll also spend weeks on revisions with someone in a different time zone who doesn't know what an Irvine business needs. Local teams cost more — and are worth it.

**SEO Built-In vs. Bolted On**
A site built with SEO from the start costs less to rank than retrofitting SEO onto a site built without it. Ask any agency if SEO is part of the build or an add-on.

## What About Ongoing Costs?

Beyond the build, plan for:
- **Hosting:** $30–$150/month (managed hosting keeps your site fast and secure)
- **SEO retainer:** $500–$2,500/month for ongoing local SEO
- **Maintenance:** $100–$300/month for updates, backups, support

## The Real Question: What's a Customer Worth to You?

If a new customer is worth $2,000 to your business and your website brings in 3 new clients in its first year, it's already paid for itself. The question isn't "how do I spend the least on a website" — it's "how do I build something that works."
