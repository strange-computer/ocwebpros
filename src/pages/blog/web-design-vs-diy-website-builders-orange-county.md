Wix. Squarespace. GoDaddy Website Builder. They make it look easy — drag, drop, done. So why do so many Orange County businesses end up hiring a professional web designer anyway?

## What DIY Builders Are Good At

If you need a basic online presence in a weekend, tools like Wix or Squarespace genuinely work. They're affordable ($15–$50/month), require no coding, and have decent templates. For a freelancer or a brand new business testing the market — a DIY site is a perfectly reasonable starting point.

## Where DIY Falls Short for Growing Businesses

**SEO is severely limited.** DIY platforms generate bloated code, slow page loads, and rigid site structures. Core Web Vitals scores on DIY sites are typically poor — and that directly affects rankings.

**You look like everyone else.** Templates are used by thousands of businesses. Custom design means your site actually reflects your brand.

**It doesn't scale.** As your business grows and you need location pages, a blog, booking integrations — DIY platforms hit walls fast.

**Your time has value.** Business owners who "built their own site" usually spent 40+ hours doing it. At any reasonable hourly rate, a professional build is often cheaper than DIY.

## Our Honest Take

If you're pre-revenue and just need a placeholder — start with Squarespace. Get to market fast.

If you're an established OC business that needs to compete in local search, convert visitors, and build something that grows with you — a custom site is the move.
